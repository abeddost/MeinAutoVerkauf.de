# How It Works

## Step 1: Vehicle Data Submission

The process starts with structured vehicle data capture, including core technical details, condition indicators, ownership context, and contact information required for evaluation and coordination.

## Step 2: Vehicle Assessment and Routing

Submitted data is reviewed through a defined **Fahrzeugbewertung** logic. Based on data quality, market relevance, and transaction criteria, the case is routed into the dealer-network process for further offer development.

## Step 3: Offer Handling

Offers are generated and organized in a consistent format so sellers can compare terms within a controlled decision framework. Offer handling is process-driven, with clear state transitions and documented communication points.

## Step 4: Transaction Coordination

After offer alignment, transaction coordination covers documentation support, handover planning, and completion tracking. The objective is a reliable transition from intent to completed sale in the German used car context.

## Selling Pathway Handling

The same underlying workflow supports:

- fast sale when speed and immediate completion are prioritized
- auction sale when competitive bidding dynamics are suitable
- dealer sale when direct dealer matching is the preferred route

These pathways are operational modes inside one governance structure, not separate disconnected products.

## Process Governance

Process governance focuses on data consistency, transparent status handling, and communication clarity for all participants. This supports professional execution quality for both private sellers and dealers in the automotive market.
